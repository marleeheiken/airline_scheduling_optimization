crew scheduling problem. I need you to generate synthetic data that realistically 
models the tradeoff between crew cost efficiency and schedule resilience.

1. Crew Utilization Rate: The percentage of available crew hours actually scheduled 
to fly, ranging from 60% to 95%. A higher utilization rate means crews are scheduled 
more tightly with less slack time.

2. Reserve Buffer Size: The number of reserve/standby crews held per 20-city route 
network, ranging from 2 to 20 reserve crews. More reserve crews provide backup when 
disruptions hit.

Generate a CSV with 100 rows showing how these two variables affect both objectives. Use realistic patterns where:
* Higher utilization rate reduces crew operating costs but also reduces resilience — when utilization is above 85%, disruption recovery becomes exponentially harder
* Larger reserve buffer improves resilience but increases costs linearly
* There is natural noise and variation in the data (not perfectly linear) to reflect real-world unpredictability
* Some combinations are clearly inefficient (high cost AND low resilience) to create dominated points for Pareto analysis
* FAA rest constraints mean that utilization rates above 92% should show sharp reliability penalties

Format: CSV with columns:
* utilization_rate (percentage, 60–95)
* reserve_buffer (integer, 2–20)
* crew_cost_index (0–100 scale, where 100 = most expensive)
* resilience_score (0–100 scale, where 100 = most resilient)
* on_time_rate (percentage of flights arriving on time, 60–98%)
* disruption_recovery_hours (average hours to recover from a major disruption, 2–72)
* overtime_cost_multiplier (how much overtime spikes during disruptions, 1.0–4.0)

Include a mix of: efficient frontier points, clearly dominated points, and a few extreme
edge cases. Do not make the data perfectly smooth — add realistic variance. Return only 
the CSV with no explanation.
